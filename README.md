## Overview

**Exoplanet Discovery Observatory** is an interactive Tableau dashboard exploring confirmed exoplanets discovered beyond our Solar System.

Built from data published by the NASA Exoplanet Archive, the dashboard provides an accessible overview of planetary systems, discovery trends, host stars, and planetary characteristics.

The project was developed as a portfolio artifact demonstrating practical skills in:

- dashboard design
- exploratory data analysis
- scientific data visualization
- data storytelling
- Tableau Public deployment

---

## Questions Explored

This dashboard investigates several questions frequently encountered in astronomy and space-data analysis:

- How many confirmed exoplanets are represented in the dataset?
- How many host systems contain known exoplanets?
- How has the pace of discovery evolved over time?
- What planetary sizes are most frequently observed?
- Which stellar systems host the greatest numbers of planets?
- How do orbital periods relate to planetary radius?

---

## Dashboard Components

### KPI Overview

High-level indicators summarizing:

- Total Exoplanets
- Host Systems
- Discovery Methods
- Latest Discovery Year

### Exoplanet Diversity Landscape

Scatter plot exploring the relationship between:

- orbital period
- planetary radius

This view highlights the diversity of planetary systems and reveals clusters of planets sharing similar characteristics.

### Discovery Timeline

Evolution of exoplanet discoveries over time, illustrating the rapid growth of planetary science during the last decade.

### Planet Radius Distribution

Distribution of planetary radii, revealing the most common planet size ranges currently represented in the archive.

### Most Planet-Rich Systems

Comparison of stellar systems hosting the highest numbers of confirmed exoplanets.
